Mexico below the average

Falling behind on cultural participation

BY DIANA BAPTISTA

Published August 19, 2018

This year's most recent statistics about cultural participation in Mexico shows that the country hasn't advanced in the last two years in getting its citizens more involved in cultural activities.
The statistics measure how many people older than 18 years attended a movie, the theater, a dance show, a live concert or a museum in the last year.

The most common cultural activity for Mexicans is going to the movies. At the bottom of the list is attendance to a dance show, a subject in which most confessed to be interested at all. The attendance has remained stable during the last years, which shows the need to engage more Mexicans into certain activities.

The statistics released this month show obvious results, like the relation between cultural participation and the stimulus received from a very young age. They also show that people with more education and better socioeconomic status participate more in cultural activities.

Mexico vs. the world

Despite unavailable data for most recent years, polls done in Latin America and Europe between 2011 and 2013 show that Mexico was below the average cultural participation. The nearest it got was in movie theater attendance, but it was still a low number compared to the results of other countries in the region.
